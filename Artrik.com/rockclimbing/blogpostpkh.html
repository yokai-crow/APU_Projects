The Rock Climbing In Pokhara

Author - Gokul Pathak
19 January. 10 min read

Rock climbing is another adventure sport activity available in Pokhara. The Three Sisters Adventure at Methalang, Sarangkot operates it. A total of 14 people can simultaneously ascend to the top of the two boulders standing 10 meters, and 21 meters each. Despite the danger involved, a thrilling experience is guaranteed while climbing the steep boulders. Nevertheless, you can also go for natural rock climbing site in Pokhara. Another artificial rock climbing venue is situated at the International Mountain Museum. This artificial structure is 22 meters high.


They offer the only location for outdoor rock climbing in and around Pokhara. They don't use it with any commercial background, and like to preserve it the way they found it with respect for the nature and wildlife around it. The climbs have been bolted and are regularly updated by their Dutch volunteer Harry Brands. The climbs are not suitable for absolute beginners and open for climbers who have already gained knowledge and skills in outdoor climbing. None of the climbs are easy for starters. They also provides facilites for climbing permits, guides and equipment. They have spent a lot time, money to rent the land, and to equip the rock with bolts. Therefore they request climbers to pay a small climbing fee to help them to maintain their rock. Best time of the year October up to June.
